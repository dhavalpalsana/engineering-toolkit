Physics & Mathematics of Coupled Cable States

Calculators often assume constant resistances at pre-set temperatures. Standard loads generate internal Joule heating which alters wire resistance. This application runs an iterative solver using 1D Radial Thermodynamics coupled with electrodynamics.

1. Electrodynamics & Thermal Coupling

Metals experience increased resistance as free electron collisions rise under temperature:

R(T) = R20 [1 + α (T - 20)]

Where α is the thermal resistance coefficient (Cu: 0.00393 K-1, Al: 0.00403 K-1).

2. Radial Heat Dissipation Path

Heat generated is defined as P = I2R. The heat flow traverses insulation conduction and ambient air/conduit convection/radiation:

Rinsulation = ln(Douter / dcond) / (2π · kinsulation)
Rconvection = 1 / (h · π · Douter)

3. Multi-Segment System Assembly

For systems with multiple spliced wire gauges, the solver calculates local heat dissipation characteristics for each segment individually. It aggregates the total electrical impedance arrays for global voltage drops, and extracts the thermal bottleneck (the segment reaching maximum temperature) for safety limit evaluation.

Designed for mechanical, industrial & aerospace cable routing configurations. Single-file offline mode enabled.
