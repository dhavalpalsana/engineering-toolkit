Conducting an Ishikawa (Fishbone) Analysis

The Ishikawa diagram (invented by Kaoru Ishikawa in 1968) is a structured tool to identify, explore, and organize the potential causes of a key problem or event (the "effect").

The 6 M's of Manufacturing

Manpower: People involved in the process.

Machine: Tools, computers, and instrumentation.

Material: Raw materials, data feeds, and inputs.

Method: Standard operating procedures and steps.

Measurement: Audits, calibrations, and inspectors.

Milieu: Environment, temperature, humidity, lighting.

The 5 P's of Software

People: Development team, QA, operators.

Process: Agile steps, reviews, CI/CD pipelines.

Product: The code base, architecture, libraries.

Platform: Cloud instances, OS, infrastructure.

Place: Environment, local developer variables.

Best Practices

  • Clearly specify the **effect** (problem statement) first. Make it measurable.
  • Conduct brainstorming sessions. Focus on listing *potential* causes before evaluating their validity.
  • Combine the Fishbone with **5-Whys** analysis: ask "Why did this happen?" repeatedly to dig deeper.
  • Look for causes that appear in multiple categories; these are likely core contributors.
