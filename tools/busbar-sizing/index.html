DC Resistance & Geometry

The DC resistance per unit length at operating temperature is the foundation for all other calculations.
R_DC(T) = ρ₀ × [1 + α × (T_op − 20)] / A
ρ₀Resistivity at 20°C: Cu = 1.7241×10⁻⁸ Ω·m, Al = 2.826×10⁻⁸ Ω·m
αTemperature coefficient: Cu = 0.00393/°C, Al = 0.00403/°C
T_opOperating temperature = T_amb + ΔT [°C]
ACross-section = W × T [m²]
Weight = ρ_density × W × T [kg/m] Cu: 8890 kg/m³ · Al: 2700 kg/m³
2

AC Skin Effect — Rac/Rdc

At AC, current density is higher at the conductor surface. The classical slab formula gives the skin effect factor k_s = Rac/Rdc, exact for infinite slabs, accurate for flat bars where W >> T.
δ = √[ρ / (π × f × μ₀)] [skin depth, metres] u = T / (2δ) [normalised half-thickness] k_s = u × [sinh(2u) + sin(2u)] / [cosh(2u) − cos(2u)]
δSkin depth at frequency f and temperature T [m]
μ₀4π × 10⁻⁷ H/m (permeability of free space)
uRatio of half-thickness to skin depth
k_sAC/DC resistance ratio (always ≥ 1.0)
3

Thermal Current Capacity & Convection

At thermal equilibrium, heat generated by I²R equals heat dissipated by convection and radiation. Given target ΔT, solve directly for I.
I² × R_AC = Q_conv + Q_rad Natural Convection (CDA empirical formula): Q_conv = C_c × P^0.75 × ΔT^1.25 [W/m] (C_c = 1.71 for vertical, 1.21 for horizontal orientation) Forced Convection (Airflow - Jurges formula): Q_conv = h_forced × P × ΔT [W/m] - For v ≤ 5 m/s: h_forced = 5.6 + 3.8 × v [W/(m²·K)] - For v > 5 m/s: h_forced = 7.2 × v^0.78 [W/(m²·K)] Radiation (Stefan-Boltzmann): Q_rad = 5.67×10⁻⁸ × ε × P × (T_bar⁴ − T_amb⁴) [W/m] I = √[(Q_conv + Q_rad) / R_AC]
PCooling perimeter = 2(W + T) [m] — exposed surfaces
vAirflow velocity [m/s]
εEmissivity: 0.10 (bright), 0.50 (oxidized), 0.90 (painted)
T_bar, T_ambAbsolute temperatures in Kelvin (add 273.15)
ΔTTemperature rise = T_bar − T_amb [°C]

Multiple Bars per Phase (CDA Derating)

Additional bars in parallel do not give proportional current gain due to restricted convection between bars and proximity effect. CDA derating factors apply (assumes gap = bar thickness).
I_total = I_single × k_N k₁ = 1.00 · k₂ = 1.80 · k₃ = 2.50 · k₄ = 3.20
k_NCDA multi-bar derating factor for N bars per phase
GapStandard: gap between bars = bar thickness T
5

Short-Circuit Thermal Rating — IEC 60865-1

Short-circuit heating is treated as adiabatic (all energy stored in conductor). The minimum cross-section to survive fault without exceeding the annealing temperature limit is:
A_min = I_sc × √t_f / k_th [mm²] or: I_withstand = A × k_th / √t_f [A] Peak electromagnetic force between phases: F = 2×10⁻⁷ × i_peak² × L_span / d [N] i_peak = κ × √2 × I_sc_rms (κ = 1.8 for X/R ≈ 10)
k_thMaterial constant: Cu = 141 A·s^0.5/mm², Al = 86 A·s^0.5/mm²
t_fFault duration [s], typically 0.1–3 s
dPhase centre-to-centre spacing [m]
κPeak factor = 1.8 (IEC 60909, X/R = 10)
6

Mechanical Deflection — Beam Theory

Busbar treated as a simply supported beam under uniformly distributed electromagnetic load during fault. Maximum deflection and bending stress at mid-span.
w_em = F / L_span [N/m, UDL per span] δ_max = 5×w×L⁴ / (384×E×I_2nd) [m] σ_bend = w×L² × c / (8 × I_2nd) [Pa] I_2nd = W × T³ / 12 (weak axis, vertical bar) I_2nd = T × W³ / 12 (strong axis, horizontal bar)
EYoung's modulus: Cu HC = 117 GPa, Al 1350 = 70 GPa
I_2ndSecond moment of area — axis depends on orientation
cDistance to extreme fibre = T/2 (weak) or W/2 (strong)
σ_yYield strength: Cu HC = 250 MPa, Al 1350-H19 = 165 MPa
7

Voltage Drop

Resistive voltage drop along the full busbar length at the rated continuous current, expressed as millivolts per metre and as a percentage of system voltage.
ΔV = I_cont × R_AC × L [V] ΔV_mpm = I_cont × R_AC × 1000 [mV/m] ΔV% = (ΔV / V_system) × 100 [%]
I_contContinuous current capacity [A] (from Module 3+4)
R_ACAC resistance per metre at operating temperature [Ω/m]
V_systemLine voltage or user-specified system voltage [V]
